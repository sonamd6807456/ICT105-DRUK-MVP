# Lab 05 Practice Case Brief: Smart Campus Event Hub

## Case Context
University students often miss campus events, workshops, competitions, club activities, and important announcements because event information is shared across multiple platforms such as social media, messaging groups, posters, student clubs, and lecturers. As a result, students may discover events too late, miss registration deadlines, or be unaware of opportunities that could enhance their academic and campus experience. Event organizers also face challenges in reaching students effectively and increasing participation.

## Target Users
### Primary Users
- University students looking for campus events and activities
- First-year students who are unfamiliar with university communication channels
- Exchange and international students seeking campus engagement opportunities

### Secondary Users
- Student clubs and event organizers who create and publish campus events
- Campus administrators who review, approve, update, and manage event information

## Product Direction
The proposed product is a web-based **Smart Campus Event Hub** that provides a centralized platform for discovering, searching, and viewing campus events. Students can browse event listings, search and filter events, view detailed event information, and register for activities. Event organizers can create and manage event announcements, while administrators can approve, update, or remove events to ensure that information remains accurate and up to date.

## Lab 05 Practice Purpose
This practice applies the wireframing and user interface design process to the Smart Campus Event Hub project. The objective is to design wireframes that demonstrate the core user journey, including event discovery, event management, and event registration. These wireframes will serve as the foundation for the MVP prototype developed in the following labs and ensure that the proposed solution aligns with the validated user needs identified during customer discovery.
