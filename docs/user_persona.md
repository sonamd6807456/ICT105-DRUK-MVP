
# Lab 04 - User Persona

## 1. Persona Name

Sonam, Year 1 Student

## 2. User Type

University student who wants to participate in campus events but often misses important announcements.

## 3. Background and Context

- Sonam is a first-year student who is still unfamiliar with campus life and extracurricular activities.
- She experiences the problem when trying to find information about workshops, competitions, and club events.
- The problem usually happens when event information is shared through multiple channels and announcements are received too late.
- She currently uses Instagram, Messenger groups, friends, and posters around campus to discover events.

## 4. Goals

- Stay informed about upcoming campus events and activities.
- Participate in workshops, competitions, and club events without missing important dates.
- Find event information quickly and easily from one place.
- Receive reminders and notifications before events occur.

| Pain Point | Evidence ID / Respondent | Explanation |

| Event information is scattered across many platforms. | R001, R005, R011, R014, R018 | Students rely on multiple sources, making it difficult to stay updated. |

## 6. Current Alternatives / Workarounds

| Current Alternative | Weakness / Limitation | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Friends and classmates | Information may not reach everyone. | R001, R007, R011, R014, R018 |
| University social media | Posts are easy to miss or buried among other content. | R002, R006, R010, R013, R016 |
| Student clubs | Information only reaches certain members. | R005, R009, R015, R020 |
| Posters around campus | Students may not notice them or see them too late. | R003, R008, R017 |
| Lecturers | Event information is shared inconsistently. | R004, R012 |

## 7. Design Implications

- The platform should provide a centralized source of campus event information.
- The system should support event reminders and notifications.
- Event details should be clear, accurate, and easy to access.
- The interface should be simple and easy for first-year students to use.
- The team should avoid unnecessary complex features during the MVP stage.
- The most important need for the MVP is helping students discover and track campus events efficiently.
