# Lab 04 - User Persona

## 1. Persona Name

Sonam, Year 1 Student

## 2. User Type

First-year university student who wants to participate in campus activities but often misses important event information.

## 3. Background and Context

- Sonam is a Year 1 student who is still adapting to university life and wants to become more involved in extracurricular activities.
- She experiences the problem when trying to find information about workshops, competitions, club activities, and campus events.
- The problem usually occurs because event announcements are spread across different channels and are sometimes received too late.
- She currently relies on friends, university social media pages, posters around campus, and student clubs to discover events.

## 4. Goals

- Stay updated on upcoming campus events.
- Participate in workshops, competitions, and club activities.
- Find event information quickly and easily.
- Receive reminders before important events.
- Avoid missing opportunities to learn and network with others.

## 5. Pain Points from Lab 03 Evidence

| Pain Point | Evidence ID / Respondent | Explanation |
|---|---|---|
| Event information is spread across many platforms. | R001, R005, R011, R014, R018 | Students must check multiple sources to find event information. |
| Students do not know where to search for events. | R003, R008, R013, R017 | Lack of a central source creates confusion. |
| Event announcements are easy to miss. | R002, R006, R010, R016, R019 | Students often receive information too late. |
| Information is outdated or unclear. | R007, R012, R020 | Some event details are incomplete or no longer current. |
| Missing events leads to lost opportunities. | R001, R005, R010, R018 | Students miss workshops and opportunities to learn new skills. |
| Students feel disconnected from campus life. | R003, R008, R013, R019 | Missing events reduces student engagement and participation. |

## 6. Current Alternatives / Workarounds

| Current Alternative | Weakness / Limitation | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Friends and classmates | Information may not reach everyone and may arrive too late. | R001, R007, R011, R014, R018 |
| University social media | Posts are easy to overlook and buried among other content. | R002, R006, R010, R013, R016, R019 |
| Posters around campus | Students may not notice them or see them after the event. | R003, R008, R017 |
| Student clubs | Information only reaches active members. | R005, R009, R015, R020 |
| Lecturers | Event information is shared inconsistently. | R004, R012 |

## 7. Design Implications

- The platform should provide a centralized source of campus event information.
- The MVP should include event reminders and notifications.
- Event details should be clear, accurate, and easy to access.
- Search and filter functions should help students quickly discover events.
- The interface should be simple and beginner-friendly for first-year students.
- The team should avoid unnecessary complex features during the MVP stage.
- The most important user need is timely access to reliable event information.
