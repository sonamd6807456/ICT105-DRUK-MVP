# Problem Notes

## Target Users

* University students seeking academic support and learning resources.
* First-year students adapting to university learning.
* International and exchange students who need easier access to educational materials.
* Students looking for study guides, tutoring, and academic advice.

## Incidents

* Students struggle to find reliable study materials for their courses.
* Students are unaware of available academic resources and support services.
* Important learning resources are spread across multiple platforms.
* Students miss deadlines because course information is difficult to track.
* Students spend excessive time searching for notes, tutorials, and study guides.

## Pain Points

* Difficulty accessing educational resources in one place.
* Lack of organized and up-to-date study materials.
* Limited access to peer recommendations and academic advice.
* Information is scattered across learning management systems, websites, and group chats.
* Students feel overwhelmed when managing coursework and assignments.

## Current Alternatives

* University Learning Management System (LMS)
* Google searches
* YouTube tutorials
* LINE and WhatsApp group chats
* Asking classmates, tutors, or senior students
* Educational websites and online forums

## Consequences

* Students may perform poorly in courses due to missing resources.
* Students waste time searching for study materials.
* Increased academic stress and frustration.
* Students may miss assignment deadlines and important announcements.
* Learning becomes less efficient and more difficult than necessary.
* Students may not fully utilize available academic support services.
